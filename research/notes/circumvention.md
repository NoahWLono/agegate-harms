# Circumvention research plan

## Definition

Circumvention is successful completion of the prohibited acquisition after an initial detection or block.

## Pathways to separate

- lying in self-declaration;
- borrowed or altered documents;
- proxy purchase by an older person;
- retrying with another staff member or store;
- switching to an unregulated online seller;
- cross-border purchase;
- vending or delivery loophole;
- account or token sharing;
- spoofing a face-estimation process;
- informal-market acquisition.

## Measurement challenges

Self-report may understate prohibited behaviour. Enforcement data captures only detected events. Mystery-shopping designs can raise legal and ethical issues. Platform logs may not link attempts across channels.

## Minimum evidence record

Document the initial block, subsequent pathway, time window, success definition, population, and whether the measure is attempt-level or person-level.

The CAI/GPEN youth privacy sweep is registered only as contextual evidence for weak online age controls. It should not be inserted directly into a Quebec retail circumvention parameter.
