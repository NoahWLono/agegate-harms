# Identification-access research plan

## Research question

Which lawful adult buyers lack an accepted, current, usable photo identity document at the moment of purchase, and what costs or barriers are required to obtain one?

## Do not use a single national prevalence without context

The evidence registry includes a federal GIS evaluation in which 26 percent of a selected sample of non-tax-filing seniors reported lacking picture ID. That result demonstrates a possible barrier in a vulnerable group. It is not representative of Quebec energy-drink buyers and is not a direct model estimate.

## Measures

- possession of accepted photo ID;
- whether it is current and legible;
- whether the person carries it during ordinary purchases;
- replacement fee and processing time;
- required address or supporting documents;
- distance and accessibility of service locations;
- name, gender-marker, appearance, or transliteration mismatch;
- loss, theft, homelessness, institutionalization, or migration-related barriers;
- willingness to present ID for a low-value purchase.

## Sampling

Include general adult buyers and purposive samples of groups likely to face barriers. Report them separately. Community organizations should review recruitment and interpretation.
