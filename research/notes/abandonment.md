# Lawful-adult abandonment research plan

## Definition

Abandonment occurs when a lawful adult does not complete a covered transaction because of the age-assurance workflow, before a final false-rejection decision.

## Mechanisms

- unwillingness to disclose identity or biometric information;
- no accepted document available;
- inaccessible camera, kiosk, or upload workflow;
- language or cognitive burden;
- device, bandwidth, or browser incompatibility;
- travel required for enrollment;
- queue or time cost;
- privacy or dignity objection;
- repeated technical failure;
- inability to obtain assistance.

## Measurement

Use transaction funnel data and participant follow-up. A checkout exit alone does not prove causation. Compare similar transactions without age assurance when ethically and legally possible.

Record:

- entry into verification;
- each failure and retry;
- help request;
- final exit or completion;
- alternative purchase or channel;
- reason in the participant's own words;
- time and out-of-pocket cost.

## Distributional reporting

Report overall rates and mechanism-specific results. Do not combine inability, refusal, privacy concern, and technical failure into a single unexplained number.
