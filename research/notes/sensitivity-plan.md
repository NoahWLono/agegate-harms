# Sensitivity-led evidence plan

## Purpose

Research effort should follow decision sensitivity rather than convenience. `results/evidence_priorities.csv` combines model influence with evidence weakness.

## Workflow

1. Run at least 50,000 draws with the dated scenario.
2. Filter priorities to `P0-critical` and `P1-high`.
3. Review whether the high correlation is structural, such as population scaling, or a contested policy parameter.
4. Define the exact population, threshold, channel, method, and outcome required.
5. Search for direct evidence before using contextual evidence.
6. Record contradictory estimates separately.
7. Update a parameter range only after documenting transferability and limitations.
8. Re-run the model and compare the priority ranking.

## Core outputs to protect

Evidence collection should support at least:

- minor transactions prevented;
- minor consumption prevented after substitution;
- adult adverse outcomes;
- higher-friction adult adverse outcomes;
- centralized records at risk;
- expected records exposed;
- total annual cost.

## Avoiding a sensitivity trap

A highly sensitive parameter is not automatically the most important policy issue. Some harms are morally or legally significant even when their count is small. Use sensitivity to allocate empirical research, not to decide values.
