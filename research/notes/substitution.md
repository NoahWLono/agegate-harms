# Substitution research plan

## Why it matters

Preventing a covered transaction does not establish that caffeine or sugar consumption fell. A person may switch products, channels, or purchasers.

## Pathways

- coffee or tea;
- more concentrated caffeinated products;
- sugary soft drinks;
- pre-workout or natural health products;
- a different energy-drink product outside the legal definition;
- proxy purchase;
- cross-border or online purchase;
- informal markets;
- no substitute.

## Model status

Version 0.2 uses one substitution rate per method. This is deliberately simple and highly uncertain.

## Preferred study

Follow blocked or policy-exposed purchase attempts over a defined period. Record product, caffeine and sugar content, source, price, timing, and whether the substitute would have occurred without the restriction.

## Future model

Replace the single rate with a transition matrix across product and channel states. Health modeling should use dose and frequency, not transaction counts alone.
