# False-rejection research plan

## Outcome definition

A false rejection occurs when a lawful adult attempts a covered purchase, continues through the verification workflow, and is denied because the system incorrectly determines that the person is under the threshold or cannot establish identity.

Keep false rejection separate from:

- abandonment before a decision;
- inability to start the process;
- document not supported by the system;
- suspected fraud;
- temporary technical failure;
- successful appeal after an initial denial.

## Required study design

For each method, record:

- threshold and challenge policy;
- participant age, especially near 16;
- accepted document types;
- device, image quality, lighting, bandwidth, and retry rules;
- human review and appeal availability;
- first-time versus repeat use;
- failure reason;
- time to successful completion;
- group-specific results where ethical and statistically supportable.

## Facial estimation

Use threshold-specific false-positive and false-negative measures. Mean absolute error is not enough to estimate denial at a cutoff. NIST FATE results are useful technical context, but direct transfer requires the same algorithm, threshold, image conditions, and population.

## Photo ID inspection

Separate:

- no accepted ID;
- expired or damaged ID;
- mismatch between appearance and document;
- staff uncertainty;
- refusal caused by store policy;
- refusal caused by the law.

## Recommended output

Report numerator, denominator, confidence interval, workflow, retries, appeal outcomes, and subgroup caveats. Do not report only a vendor's aggregate accuracy percentage.
