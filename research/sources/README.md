# Source management

## What belongs in Git

- bibliographic metadata;
- stable source URLs;
- short research notes in original words;
- extraction and transformation scripts;
- source hashes where useful;
- licensing and attribution information.

## What normally does not belong in Git

- copyrighted PDFs copied without redistribution permission;
- participant data;
- recordings or transcripts;
- documents containing personal or confidential information;
- vendor material received under restriction.

The ignored `research/sources/pdfs/` directory is only a local convenience. Confirm licensing before redistributing a source.

## Source review checklist

1. Retrieve the authoritative or primary source where possible.
2. Record retrieval date and stable URL.
3. Identify population, threshold, channel, and workflow.
4. Extract numerator, denominator, uncertainty, and failure definitions.
5. Identify who funded and performed the study.
6. Assess directness and transferability.
7. Record limitations before using an estimate.
8. Preserve conflicts rather than forcing consensus.
